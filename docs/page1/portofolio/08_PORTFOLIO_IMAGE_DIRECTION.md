# Introduction

Photography is the heart of the Portfolio.

Interfaces explain.

Photography convinces.

The Portfolio should be remembered through its images,
not its UI.

---

# Philosophy

Do not photograph projects.

Photograph people creating change.

The deliverable is secondary.

The journey is primary.

---

↓

# Storytelling Through Images

Every project should visually answer:

Who?

↓

Where?

↓

What happened?

↓

How did people collaborate?

↓

What changed?

A visitor should understand the project even before reading.

# Avoid

Looking directly at camera.

Award ceremonies.

Formal group photos.

Office desks.

Stock photography.

AI generated humans.

Corporate handshake photos.

Artificial poses.

Overly clean environments.

Marketing photos.

---

# Framing

Wide environmental shots.

Medium interaction shots.

Close-up detail shots.

Hands.

Notes.

Products.

Expressions.

The combination creates narrative.

---

# Composition

Primary Image

Largest.

Emotion.

↓

Secondary Image

Context.

↓

Detail Image

Evidence.

↓

Human Detail

Connection.

Never make every image equal.

---

# Cropping

Natural.

Never crop faces awkwardly.

Never crop hands during interaction.

Allow the environment to explain the story.

---

# Image Quality

High resolution.

Natural colors.

Soft contrast.

True skin tones.

Visible texture.

No heavy HDR.

No oversharpening.

---

# Editorial Sequence

Large image.

↓

Detail.

↓

↓

↓

Result.

---

# Relationship With Layout

Photography leads.

Typography follows.

UI supports.

Never let interface dominate photography.

---

# Responsive Strategy

Desktop

Editorial composition.

Tablet

Preserve hierarchy.

Mobile

One image deserves the screen.

Never create tiny unreadable thumbnails.

---

# Art Direction

Photography should feel collected over time.

Not produced in one photoshoot.

Mix

Wide documentary scenes.

Medium interactions.

Close human details.

Environmental context.

Natural imperfections are welcome.

Slightly imperfect framing often feels more authentic than perfect composition.

Every image should answer one emotional question,

not one technical question.
