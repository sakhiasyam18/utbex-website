# Introduction

The Impact section is not a statistics section.

It is the moment where stories become measurable.

Visitors should already believe the journey.

Numbers simply strengthen that belief.

Evidence follows emotion.

Never the opposite.

---

# Communication Objective

Answer one question only.

"What difference has all of this created?"

Do not present numbers without context.

Every metric should represent real people,

real communities,

and real transformation.

---

# Psychological Goal

Move visitors from confidence

↓

to certainty.

Visitors should think:

"This organization doesn't just run programs."

"They create lasting impact."

---

# Emotional Transition

Before Impact

↓

"I've seen the journey."

After Impact

↓

"I understand the results."

Impact should feel calm.

Not celebratory.

Not boastful.

Simply undeniable.

---

# Story Formula

Human Story

↓

Transformation

↓

Evidence

↓

Numbers

↓

Reflection

↓

Future

Numbers should never appear first.

---

# Tone of Voice

Quiet confidence.

Transparent.

Honest.

Responsible.

Grounded.

Avoid sounding victorious.

Impact belongs to communities,

not to UTBEX.

---

# Writing Principles

Every statistic should answer:

"So what?"

Instead of saying:

"120 Training Sessions."

Explain what those sessions enabled.

Numbers without meaning become decoration.

---

# Avoid

Do not write:

120+

50+

10+

25+

Without explanation.

Avoid:

Amazing.

Incredible.

Massive.

Outstanding.

Leading.

These words reduce credibility.

# Statistics Philosophy

Every metric should represent a human story.

Instead of displaying isolated numbers,

pair them with short contextual descriptions.

Example:

150+

Pelaku UMKM yang mendapatkan pendampingan untuk mengembangkan usahanya.

20+

Desa yang berkolaborasi dalam berbagai program pengembangan masyarakat.

95%

Mitra menyatakan kolaborasi memberikan manfaat nyata bagi komunitas mereka.

The number attracts attention.

The sentence creates meaning.

---

# Reading Speed

Headline

≈ 3 seconds

Introduction

≈ 8 seconds

Statistics

≈ 6 seconds

Reflection

≈ 5 seconds

Visitors should understand the message in under thirty seconds.

---

# Memory Hook

If visitors remember one sentence,

it should be:

"Perubahan Tidak Berhenti Saat Program Selesai."

---

# Relationship With Visual Design

Statistics should never dominate.

Whitespace remains essential.

Large typography should create confidence,

not pressure.

Icons should support meaning,

not decorate numbers.

---

# Relationship With Photography

Impact photography should show outcomes.

Not activities.

Examples:

Communities using new skills.

Products being sold.

Public exhibitions.

Collaborative achievements.

Real-life continuation.

Photography should answer:

"What changed?"

---

# Relationship With Motion

Numbers may animate gently.

The animation should feel natural.

Never use dramatic counting effects.

Impact should feel trustworthy,

not theatrical.

---

# Accessibility

Every metric must remain understandable for screen readers.

Avoid presenting information only through large numbers.

Context is mandatory.

---

# Validation Checklist

✓ Human-first.

✓ Evidence-based.

✓ Calm.

✓ Honest.

✓ Memorable.

✓ Indonesian-first.

✓ Contextual.

✓ Future-oriented.

---

# Final Principle

Visitors should never leave remembering:

"UTBEX reached 500 people."

Instead,

they should remember:

"UTBEX creates change that continues long after the program ends."

When numbers become stories,

the Impact section has succeeded.
