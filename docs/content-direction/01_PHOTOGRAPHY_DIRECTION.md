# Introduction

Photography is the strongest storytelling medium of the UTBEX website.

Visitors trust photographs before they trust words.

Every image should feel like evidence.

Not decoration.

Not illustration.

Not stock photography.

Photography is the documentary language of UTBEX.

---

# Philosophy

The website should never feel like a corporate brochure.

Instead,

it should feel like opening a visual documentary about communities,

innovation,

education,

and transformation.

Every photograph should answer one silent question:

"What actually happened here?"

---

# Human First

People always come before projects.

Projects exist because people exist.

Whenever possible,

photography should prioritize:

people

↓

interaction

↓

emotion

↓

activity

↓

environment

Never reverse this order.

---

# Authenticity

Every photograph should feel genuine.

Allowed:

Real workshops.

Real mentoring.

Real discussions.

Real field activities.

Real collaboration.

Real celebrations.

Real documentation.

Avoid:

Artificial posing.

Corporate handshakes.

Stock-style smiles.

Generic business meetings.

Fake office culture.

---

# Emotional Direction

Preferred emotions:

Curiosity.

Focus.

Hope.

Collaboration.

Learning.

Pride.

Achievement.

Community.

Avoid exaggerated excitement.

Natural expressions build more trust.

---

# Photography Categories

Every image should belong to one category.

Category A

Hero Image

The strongest emotional image.

Represents the entire story.

Only one hero image should dominate a visual sequence.

---

Category B

Environment Shot

Shows location.

Provides context.

Creates atmosphere.

Examples:

Village.

Classroom.

Community hall.

Workshop.

Outdoor activities.

---

Category C

Human Interaction

People teaching.

People discussing.

People collaborating.

People creating.

These images build emotional connection.

---

Category D

Detail Shot

Hands.

Notebook.

Laptop.

Products.

Tools.

Signage.

Materials.

Small details make stories believable.

---

Category E

Achievement

Awards.

Certificates.

Government partnerships.

Media exposure.

Billboards.

Public recognition.

These images validate credibility.

---

Category F

Celebration

Group photos.

Successful events.

Community gatherings.

Graduation.

Launches.

Milestones.

These images conclude a story.

---

# Hero Photography

Hero photography should feel iconic.

Requirements:

Strong composition.

Clean background.

Natural lighting.

Clear focal point.

Authentic subject.

Hero images should immediately communicate purpose.

---

# Editorial Photography

Not every photograph deserves equal importance.

Some images should dominate.

Others should quietly support.

The editorial hierarchy controls emotional rhythm.

---

# Composition

Preferred compositions:

Rule of thirds.

Natural framing.

Leading lines.

Negative space.

Human-centered focus.

Avoid overly symmetrical compositions unless intentional.

---

# Crop Direction

Never crop randomly.

Protect:

Faces.

Hands.

Interaction.

Eye contact.

Important context.

Every crop should preserve the story.

---

# Eye Direction

Whenever possible,

allow subjects to naturally guide the visitor's reading direction.

People looking toward the next section subtly encourage scrolling.

Eye direction becomes invisible navigation.

---

# Color Consistency

Photography should share one visual language.

Warm.

Natural.

Balanced.

Soft contrast.

True skin tones.

Avoid:

Heavy filters.

Oversaturation.

Extreme HDR.

Artificial color grading.

---

# Lighting

Prefer natural light.

Soft window light.

Golden hour.

Overcast daylight.

Gentle indoor lighting.

Avoid harsh flash.

Avoid dramatic studio lighting.

The website should feel human,

not commercial.

---

# Image Hierarchy

Within a story,

only one image becomes the visual leader.

The remaining images support it.

Example:

Hero Image

↓

Supporting Context

↓

Human Detail

↓

Achievement

↓

Closing Moment

Visitors should naturally understand where to look first.

---

# Image Variety

Avoid repeating similar photographs.

Alternate:

Portrait.

Landscape.

Wide.

Close.

Crowded.

Minimal.

Calm.

Dynamic.

Visual variation creates editorial quality.

---

# Sequence

Photographs should create progression.

Beginning.

↓

Learning.

↓

Action.

↓

Result.

↓

Celebration.

↓

Reflection.

Images should never appear randomly.

---

# Relationship With Typography

Photography attracts attention.

Typography explains meaning.

Never allow typography to cover important visual information.

Respect the photograph.

---

# Responsive Behaviour

Every crop should be reviewed independently.

Desktop.

Tablet.

Mobile.

The emotional focus must survive every screen size.

---

# Accessibility

Every content image requires meaningful alt text.

Alt text should describe:

What is happening.

Who is involved.

Why it matters.

Avoid generic descriptions.

---

# Photography Checklist

✓ Real.

✓ Human.

✓ Documentary.

✓ Emotional.

✓ Editorial.

✓ Authentic.

✓ Warm.

✓ Premium.

✓ Consistent.

---

# Final Principle

A visitor should never think,

"These are beautiful photos."

Instead,

they should think,

"These are real people doing meaningful work."

When photography creates trust before a single paragraph is read,

the UTBEX Photography Direction has succeeded.
