# Introduction

The website is not a gallery.

It is not a project archive.

It is not a company profile.

The website is a documentary.

Visitors should experience one continuous journey.

Every image,

every paragraph,

every statistic,

and every section should naturally answer the previous one.

---

# Philosophy

Stories are remembered.

Lists are forgotten.

Never present projects as isolated achievements.

Instead,

present them as chapters of one larger mission.

---

# Story Arc

Every visitor should unconsciously experience:

Discovery

↓

Understanding

↓

Trust

↓

Evidence

↓

Transformation

↓

Impact

↓

Conversation

Every page should preserve this emotional sequence.

---

# Documentary Structure

The entire website follows one narrative.

Chapter 01

Who are we?

↓

Chapter 02

Why does this matter?

↓

Chapter 03

What actually happened?

↓

Chapter 04

Who benefited?

↓

Chapter 05

What changed?

↓

Chapter 06

Where are we going next?

Visitors should never feel that chapters are disconnected.

---

# Hero

Purpose:

Create curiosity.

Reveal purpose.

Present identity.

Do not explain everything.

Leave visitors wanting to continue.

Hero ends with a question,

not an answer.

---

# About

Purpose:

Build understanding.

Introduce philosophy.

Introduce values.

Introduce people.

Visitors begin trusting the organization.

---

# Documentary Break

Introduce one full-width documentary photograph.

No statistics.

No cards.

No distractions.

Just one meaningful image.

This functions as a visual breath before entering the work itself.

---

# Portfolio

Portfolio is not a showcase.

Portfolio is evidence.

Every project should answer:

What problem existed?

↓

What action was taken?

↓

Who participated?

↓

What changed?

Never begin with awards.

Always begin with people.

---

# Internal Story Order

Within every project:

Environment

↓

Preparation

↓

Workshop

↓

Discussion

↓

Implementation

↓

Community

↓

Result

↓

Reflection

↓

Recognition

Never reverse this order.

Recognition validates.

It never introduces.

---

# Image Narrative

The project already contains multiple types of photographs.

Arrange them intentionally.

Preferred order:

Wide environmental photograph

↓

Human interaction

↓

Educational activity

↓

Close-up details

↓

Community engagement

↓

Achievement

↓

Celebration

↓

Reflection

Visitors should feel time progressing.

---

# Full Width Documentary Panels

The panoramic photographs should become chapter separators.

Never reduce them into cards.

Never crop them heavily.

Allow them to occupy almost the entire viewport.

Purpose:

Pause.

Reflect.

Continue.

---

# Impact

Impact should feel inevitable.

Visitors should already believe the story before seeing statistics.

Statistics simply confirm what photography has already demonstrated.

Evidence first.

Numbers second.

---

# Footer

Footer is not another section.

Footer is the closing page.

Visitors should feel calm.

Satisfied.

Curious about future collaboration.

Never introduce new information here.

Only conclude.

---

# Story Rhythm

Alternate between:

Large moment

↓

Quiet moment

↓

Evidence

↓

Reflection

↓

Large moment

↓

Silence

↓

Conversation

Avoid constant intensity.

---

# Emotional Timeline

Curiosity

↓

Interest

↓

Trust

↓

Engagement

↓

Evidence

↓

Confidence

↓

Belonging

↓

Action

Every visual decision should reinforce this timeline.

---

# Photography Placement

Avoid clustering similar photographs.

Do not display:

Five workshop photos together.

Five award photos together.

Five group photos together.

Instead alternate visual subjects.

This maintains freshness.

---

# Recognition Placement

Awards.

Certificates.

Government collaboration.

Billboards.

Media publications.

These should appear near the end.

Recognition is the consequence of meaningful work,

not the beginning of the story.

---

# Closing Image

The final documentary photograph should leave emotional resonance.

Not excitement.

Not marketing.

Reflection.

Visitors should quietly feel:

"I would like to become part of this journey."

---

# Future Expansion

Every future project should naturally fit this storytelling framework.

Never create independent project stories.

Every project becomes another chapter inside the larger UTBEX documentary.

---

# Final Principle

Visitors should never remember individual projects.

They should remember one continuous journey of people,

ideas,

collaboration,

and lasting impact.

That is the purpose of Story Sequence.
