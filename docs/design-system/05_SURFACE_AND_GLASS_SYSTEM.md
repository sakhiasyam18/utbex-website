---

# Introduction

Surfaces define hierarchy.

Glass creates atmosphere.

Neither should become decoration.

The visitor should experience depth naturally,

without consciously noticing the interface.

---

# Philosophy

Every surface belongs to one visual ecosystem.

Solid surfaces provide stability.

Glass surfaces provide separation.

Light provides depth.

Together they create a premium editorial experience.

---

# Surface Hierarchy

Level 01

Primary Canvas

Pure white.

The foundation of the website.

---

Level 02

Elevated Surface

Very soft neutral background.

Used for cards,

information blocks,

and supporting content.

---

Level 03

Glass Surface

Floating layer.

Navigation.

Floating cards.

Contextual overlays.

Never overused.

---

Level 04

Modal Surface

Highest visual elevation.

Reserved only for dialogs or future interactive experiences.

---

# Glass Philosophy

Glass is not an effect.

Glass is a layer.

Its purpose is to separate content gently,

while maintaining visual continuity.

---

# Glass Characteristics

Required:

• soft backdrop blur

• translucent white

• thin translucent border

• subtle internal highlight

• premium shadow

Optional:

• ambient reflection

• warm light diffusion

• gentle radial lighting

---

# Surface Lighting

Every elevated surface should receive light before shadow.

Use:

Soft reflections.

Top highlights.

Ambient diffusion.

Light creates realism.

Shadow reinforces it.

---

# Reflection System

Glass may contain:

• subtle top reflection

• faint edge highlight

• soft internal glow

Never create obvious glossy effects.

Reflections should almost disappear.

---

# Shadow System

Large blur.

Low opacity.

Wide spread.

Natural elevation.

Never use harsh shadows.

Never create floating black boxes.

---

# Ambient Depth

To avoid flat interfaces,

introduce invisible atmosphere.

Allowed:

• radial white lighting

• warm neutral diffusion

• soft maroon ambient glow

• blurred background light

Opacity should remain extremely low.

The atmosphere should be felt,

never seen.

---

# Blur Philosophy

Blur improves separation.

Not decoration.

Background content should remain recognizable.

Never create cloudy glass.

Maintain readability at all times.

---

# Border System

Glass borders should feel like light,

not outlines.

Recommended:

1px translucent border.

Very soft gradient.

Subtle edge illumination.

---

# Rounded Corners

Use consistent radius.

Large enough to feel modern.

Never exaggerated.

Glass should feel refined,

not playful.

---

# Responsive Behaviour

Glass appearance remains consistent.

Only size changes.

Never simplify the visual language on smaller screens.

---

# Surface Checklist

✓ Premium.

✓ Floating.

✓ Calm.

✓ Lightweight.

✓ Layered.

✓ Atmospheric.

✓ Elegant.

✓ Consistent.

---

# Final Principle

Surfaces should quietly organize the experience.

Glass should quietly connect the layers.

Light should quietly bring everything to life.

Visitors should remember the atmosphere,

not the effects.
