---

# Introduction

The Footer should say less,

but mean more.

It is not a place for explanation.

It is a place for reflection.

---

# Content Philosophy

Every sentence must have a purpose.

Remove anything that sounds like marketing.

Remove anything that repeats previous sections.

Keep only what naturally closes the story.

---

# Closing Message

One editorial headline.

One supporting paragraph.

Nothing more.

The closing message should invite,

not persuade.

---

# Editorial Tone

Quiet.

Confident.

Human.

Hopeful.

Timeless.

Never corporate.

Never promotional.

Never exaggerated.

---

# Navigation Labels

Use short labels.

One or two words only.

Examples

Home

About

Portfolio

Contact

Never use long navigation titles.

---

# Contact Information

Present information clearly.

No unnecessary descriptions.

Examples

Email

Location

Working Hours (optional)

Phone (optional)

Keep everything minimal.

---

# Social Content

Only official channels.

Do not encourage followers.

Do not use promotional copy.

Social media exists for continuity,

not marketing.

---

# Copyright

Simple.

Readable.

Quiet.

Example

© 2026 UTBEX Indonesia.

All rights reserved.

Nothing decorative.

---

# Writing Style

Short sentences.

Comfortable reading rhythm.

No buzzwords.

No hype.

No unnecessary adjectives.

Every word earns its place.

---

# Reading Experience

Headline.

↓

Pause.

↓

Paragraph.

↓

Navigation.

↓

Contact.

↓

Leave.

Visitors should finish reading in less than one minute.

---

# Content Checklist

✓ Human.

✓ Editorial.

✓ Calm.

✓ Honest.

✓ Minimal.

✓ Easy to scan.

✓ Consistent with Hero, About, and Portfolio.

---

# Final Principle

The Footer should leave visitors with one final thought:

Every meaningful collaboration begins with a simple conversation.

Nothing more needs to be said.
