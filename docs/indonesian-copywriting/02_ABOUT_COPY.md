# Introduction

The About section is where visitors decide whether UTBEX is trustworthy.

This section should never read like an institutional profile.

It should read like the beginning of a long-term relationship.

The visitor should feel:

"These are people who genuinely understand the challenges they are solving."

---

# Communication Objective

Answer one question only.

"Why does UTBEX exist?"

Do not explain every program.

Do not list achievements.

Explain the purpose behind the work.

Purpose builds trust.

Programs can be explored later.

---

# Psychological Goal

Visitors should transition from curiosity to confidence.

The About section should quietly communicate:

"We understand the problem."

"We work alongside communities."

"We create lasting impact."

Trust should emerge naturally.

Never force it.

---

# Tone of Voice

Professional.

Human.

Calm.

Optimistic.

Grounded.

Never sound defensive.

Never sound arrogant.

Never exaggerate impact.

---

# Writing Principles

Write as if speaking to a future collaborator.

Not to a customer.

Not to a government official.

Not to an investor.

Everyone should feel equally welcomed.

---

# Avoid

Do not write:

"Kami adalah lembaga..."

"Kami memiliki komitmen..."

"Kami memberikan solusi terbaik..."

"Kami terpercaya..."

"Kami berpengalaman..."

Visitors should discover these qualities through the story.

---

# Preferred Narrative

Start with people.

Continue with collaboration.

End with impact.

Never reverse this order.

# Recommended Body Copy

UTBEX lahir dari keyakinan bahwa perubahan tidak harus selalu dimulai dari sesuatu yang besar.

Bersama desa, UMKM, komunitas, dan berbagai mitra, kami membangun ruang untuk belajar, berkolaborasi, dan menciptakan solusi yang tumbuh dari kebutuhan.

Bagi kami, keberhasilan bukan hanya tentang menyelesaikan sebuah program, tetapi tentang memastikan dampaknya terus dirasakan setelah perjalanan itu dimulai.

# Relationship With Photography

Photography should reinforce the message.

Preferred subjects:

Discussion.

Mentoring.

Learning.

Field activities.

Human interaction.

Avoid:

Award ceremonies.

Certificates.

Formal documentation.

Recognition belongs later.

---

# Relationship With Layout

The About copy should never dominate the screen.

Photography carries emotion.

Copy provides meaning.

Maintain generous whitespace.

Allow visitors to breathe.

Avoid complex terminology.

The copy should remain understandable even when skimmed.
